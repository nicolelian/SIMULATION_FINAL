Introducing our Influence Diagram:

This diagram will give you an insight into all the key elements in our restaurant that will later help you in picturing our decisions, uncertainties that we accounted for and the objective of embarking on this simulation project.
Let's take you through one particular scenario:

  • The number of customers in the pokemon restaurant increases
  • Very intuitively, you know that the number of seats available automatically decreases
  • You also know that the availability of the cashier decreases
  • As the capacity of the restaurant fills up, the rejected customers also increases
  • As the capacity of the restaurant fills up, the queue gets longer
  • Therefore, the time spent in the restaurant also increases




    • Next, let's take a deep dive into how the back end works by looking at our modal analysis:

    This diagram shows you the entire flow of a pokemon customer's experience in our Alfresco restaurant. Our agent-based simulation model has been very purposely divided into 8 different states.

    Let's take you through each state:

    Upon even arriving, unlike the doctor and patient non-colliding model, their possibility of entry is not determined at the cashier. The customer decides at that point based on a visual intake if the restaurant is full or if they would like to stand in the queue to wait.

    If they don't they immediately go to "rejected"

    Else, what happens is that they enter the restarurant and go from unordered to waiting when they enter the queue. Just like the non-colliding model, when they are the next customer, they will enter the staging area.

    When they are at the cashier, change to ordering and after this, they have 2 options. one of which is to go to their table to eat the food they have served, categorized as eating . Or proceed to the drinks dispenser which is represented by ordering 2 to collect their drinks before heading to the table to eat.

    After they have completed their meal, eaten they move on to the exit and get removed from the system.
