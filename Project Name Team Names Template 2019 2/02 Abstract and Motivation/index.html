Abstract & Motivation

The main goal of our project was to develop a simulation model that tied in very closely to the crooked model. Therefore, it ties in very closely to a normal restaurant. The only difference would be that the drinks queue, like crooked cooks is separate from the cashier queue.

Why did we decide to embark on this simulation?

This is because we wanted to find out what was the optimal number of tables that needs to be present in the restaruant for restaurant to constantly have positive revenue.

When does one decide if they want to enter or exit the restaruant?

  • When the capacity of the restaurant is full and there are no longer any available seats
  • When the queue exceeds a certain amount of people

    • We will be simulating this based on the non-collliding model that was went through in class. We collected data on tables from 1 to 10. In order to ensure that we simulate it as realistic as possile based on customer psyche, we assume that Customers will not want to enter the restaurant beyond a certain queue length. That is why our queue length is not equivalent to the seat capacity in our restaurant.
